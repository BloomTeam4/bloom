Now, let me tell you a bit about air quality, and how important it really is.

Why should we care about air pollution anyways?

Well, according to Mackenzie's 2016 study, air pollution in the form of carbon dioxide and methane from natural and industrial sources raise the earth’s temperature. These gases are known as greenhouse gases, which trap the Earth’s heat in the atmosphere, which in turn lead to global warming and climate change.

It's a bigger health risk than you might think...

Not only does air pollution affect the Earth’s temperatures, but they directly affect our health as well. An air pollutant, benzene, is classified as a carcinogen and can cause lung irritation in the short term and blood disorders in the long term.

Those crowded cities and trafficked suburbs are hotspots for outdoor air pollution.

According to the World Health Organization, 97% of cities in low- and middle-income countries with more than 100,000 inhabitants do not meet the minimum air quality levels. Around 4M of the approximately 7M people who die from air pollution-related diseases every year live in the Asia-Pacific region.

So, how exactly can we put an end to air pollution for the betterment of future generations?

We can reduce emissions from transportation. And no, that doesn’t mean you have to go out and buy a fancy electric car (although, they are great for the environment). We can reduce emissions by walking, biking, or taking public transportation.

The little things add up!

We can also reduce emissions by turning off lights and appliances when they are not in use or even by using energy efficient light bulbs and appliances.
